\chapter{Introduction}

The EEG signal has been used in both research and clinical applications to classify brain states and diagnose various conditions, like epilepsy or sleep disorders\cite{jain}.
What was initially manual inspection is getting superseded by automated computer methods.
The ability to infer signals from EEG recordings has sparked hope for eventual use of EEG in Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI).
Our work explores one such avenue.

We analyze datasets of EEG recordings while participants listened to music.
We attempt to infer music properties from the EEG signals with machine learning methods.
We succeed at song classification task but observe poor across subject generalization.
We try to explain this result with an experiment that attempts to perform song identification using a classical DTW algorithm.

We attempt emotion classification, which, albeit being similar to song identification, shows poor results. 
We also define a novel classification task based on number of note onsets in a fragment of musical recording and solve it with our methods.

Lastly, we tackle the harder than classification problem of music decoding from EEG and achieve results good enough to perform classification on, but the poor generalization problem persists.
